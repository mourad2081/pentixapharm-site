# Only 3 Insurances are initially critical

Germany is notorious for its love of paperwork and insurance coverage. If you just arrived, don't let brokers sell you a package of 15 different policies. You really only need these three immediately:

### 1. Health Insurance (Krankenversicherung) 
This is **legally mandatory**. You cannot get a work visa without it. Depending on your salary, you either join the public system (TK, AOK) or opt for a private insurer (PKV). 

### 2. Personal Liability Insurance (Privathaftpflicht)
In Germany, if you accidentally break someone's laptop, bump a pedestrian on your bike, or cause water damage to your rented apartment, you are liable with your entire private fortune. This insurance costs around 50€ **per year** and covers damages up to 50 Million Euros. It's the ultimate no-brainer.

### 3. Occupational Disability Insurance (Berufsunfähigkeitsversicherung / BU)
What happens if burnout, an accident, or a severe illness prevents you from doing your desk job? The state pays you a tiny fraction of your income. The BU steps in and pays your monthly salary until you retire. In Germany, your ability to work is your biggest financial asset. Protect it.
