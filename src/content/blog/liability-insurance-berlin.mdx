If there is one piece of advice every expat hears within 24 hours of landing at BER, it’s this: **"Get a Haftpflichtversicherung."**

In many parts of the world, personal liability insurance is seen as something for businesses or homeowners. But in Germany, according to the Civil Code (BGB § 823), you are liable for damages you cause to others with **all your current and future assets.**

Here are 3 specific Berlin scenarios where not having one could ruin your financial future.

### 1. The 'Lost Key' Nightmare
Living in an Altbau in Prenzlauer Berg or Neukölln? Your house keys often belong to a complex central locking system. If you lose your keys, the landlord might decide to replace the **entire locking system** for the whole building for security reasons.
*   **The Cost:** €2,000 to €15,000.
*   **The Solution:** A standard Private Liability (Haftpflicht) from ERGO covers "Key Loss" by default.

### 2. The Bicycle Collision
Berlin is a bike city. If you accidentally hit a tourist or another cyclist while riding through Friedrichshain, and they suffer an injury that prevents them from working, you are liable for their medical costs **plus their lost wages.**
*   **The Cost:** Potentially hundreds of thousands in "Schmerzensgeld" (pain and suffering) and long-term care.
*   **The Solution:** Unlimited coverage for personal injury up to €50 Million ensures you never have to file for bankruptcy over an accident.

### 3. The 'Airbnb' or Sublet Mishap
Many expats start their journey in temporary accommodation. If you accidentally leave the faucet running in your temporary apartment and flood the neighbor below, the building insurance will come looking for *you* to pay the damages.
*   **The Cost:** Damage to furniture, flooring, and structural dampness. Usually €5,000+.
*   **The Solution:** Coverage for "Damages to Rented Property" is a standard but vital pillar of our plans.

### Conclusion
At less than €5 per month, there is zero reason not to have this protection. It is the cheapest piece of "peace of mind" you can buy in Germany.
